About Me

Hi, my name is Maurice and I'm a data enthusiast living in Berlin. I'm very much a numbers guy and someone who loves to make new discoveries in a thing that we all know but only a few love - Data. I created this site primarily for myself to store all kinds of topics that interest me in a single space. Here I can write about them and use it as a way to deepen my knowledge. And who knows...maybe someone else will learning something from it too. :)
But now some information about myself. I do hold a Physics degree from the Technical University of Berlin. One half of my time is spent on my master studies where I mainly focus on theoretical physics and numerical simulations and its applications in Astro- and Plasmaphysics or Medicine. Things like Kelvin-Helmholtz and Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities or the biofluidmechanics of our circulatory system and artificial organs. My bachelor's thesis was written on the calculations of stellar trajectories using numerical integration methods. A small abstract can be found on the website for the virtual annual meeting of the German Astronomical Society. Currently the other half of my professional time is spent on my work for Siemens as a Data Analyst, Software Developer and Technical Projectmanager where we develop a cloud-implemented automated data pipeline to extract and analyse gigabytes of uploaded data. Before joining Siemens, I was mainly working on topics like NLP and Genetic Algorithms for companies like Motionlogic or MIA MEDICAL INFORMATION ANALYTICS.
